The Risk of Advance Vacation

Taking vacation days before you've earned them is possible through an agreement, but it creates a debt. This debt has significant long-term implications you should be aware of.

Resignation Risk Scenario

Let's see what happens if you take advance leave and then resign before the debt is cleared.

Step 1: Take Advance Vacation

You take 20 days of advance vacation in your first year.

Step 2: Use Newly Earned Days

The next year, you use all 24 of your available days during the summer.

Step 3: Resign from Your Position

You resign in August. At this point, you have only earned a few new vacation days (from April to August).

Outcome: Potential Repayment

The small number of days you earned from April-August is not enough to cover the original 20-day debt. The remaining debt is deducted from your final salary, and you may even be required to pay back the difference.
